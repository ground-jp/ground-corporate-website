Our Journey

The Story of GROUND

EST. 2015 / 10 YEARS OF CHALLENGE

10 years of transforming logistics through technology

The logistics industry has long faced structural challenges of labor shortages and manual-dependent operations. GROUND was founded in 2015 to fundamentally solve these challenges through AI, mathematical optimization, and robotics. Starting with overseas robot distribution, then evolving into proprietary AI software development, and finally the birth of "GWES" — the integrated logistics facility management system. This decade-long journey is the story of logistics DX itself.

CHAPTER 0
Prehistory
2012–2014
2012
Amazon Acquires Kiva Systems for $775M
The era dawns when robotics becomes central to warehouse operations. The urgent need for similar technological innovation in Japan's logistics industry becomes clear.
INDUSTRY
2012–2014
Rakuten Logistics and Hitachi Jointly Develop Logistics Robot
Prototypes are repeated and presented as "Racrew" in 2014. Japan's journey toward practical application of logistics robotics begins. This experience and knowledge eventually leads to GROUND's founding.
PREHISTORY
CHAPTER 1
Founding & Challenge
2015–2019
2015.04
GROUND Corporation Founded
Founded to realize "Intelligent Logistics" by fusing technology and logistics. Drawing on experience at Rakuten Logistics and the momentum of rapid logistics robotics advancement overseas, we launched as Japan's logistics tech company.
Founded
2016.01
Strategic Partnership with GreyOrange
Established strategic partnership with Indian robotics company GreyOrange. Acquired exclusive distribution rights in Japan for logistics robot "Butler®".
Partnership
2016.05
Strategic Partnership with Okamura
Established strategic partnership with Okamura, a leader in office and logistics equipment. Began joint promotion of logistics systems leveraging robot technology. The beginning of a fateful relationship to be strengthened a decade later.
Partnership
2017.06
Strategic Partnership with Daiwa House Industry
Concluded strategic partnership with Daiwa House Industry, a leading logistics facility company. Received 1 billion yen investment to accelerate robot deployment in logistics facilities.
Funding
2017.12
Butler® First Domestic Deployment — Nitori Group
Based on supply contract with furniture and interior retailer Nitori Group, logistics robot "Butler®" was deployed domestically for the first time. A new era for Japan's logistics robotics dawns.
Deployment
2018.07
AI Logistics Software "DyAS" Pilot Project
Began pilot testing of DyAS at Trusco Nakayama's distribution center. Focused on inventory placement optimization and workforce allocation efficiency, marking an important step toward transitioning from robot company to software company.
Product
2019.02
Series A Funding: 500 Million Yen
Completed Series A funding of 500 million yen, with participation from Daiwa House Industry, Okamura, and others. Solidified capital base for software business expansion.
Funding
2019.10
Executive Changes: New Management Structure
Leadership transition marks shift toward full software business model. New organizational structure established to accelerate DyAS market penetration.
Organization
CHAPTER 2
Evolution to Software
2020–2022
2020.07
DyAS First Customer Deployment
DyAS completed first commercial deployment. Successfully demonstrated warehouse optimization across multiple facilities, validating software-first strategy.
Deployment
2020.12
Merger Agreement with Okamura
Agreed to merge with Okamura under mutual strategic vision. Long-term partnership evolves into integrated entity, combining logistics software expertise with facility infrastructure.
Partnership
2021.01
Merger with Okamura Completed
Completed integration with Okamura. Became wholly-owned subsidiary of Okamura Corporation, enabling accelerated development and deployment of integrated logistics solutions.
Milestone
2021.07
GWES Concept Unveiled
Announced GWES (GROUND Warehouse Execution System) concept. Integrated logistics facility management platform unifying software, robotics, and facility management.
Product

GWES Beta Launch at Flagship Facility
GWES beta version deployed at Okamura flagship logistics facility. First integrated test of complete ecosystem combining AI software, robotic systems, and facility infrastructure.
Deployment
2022.04
GWES Commercial Release
GWES officially launched to market. First integrated logistics facility management system in Japan. Marks culmination of seven-year journey from robotics to platform.
Product
2022.10
Partnership with SX Cargo
Established partnership with SX Cargo for GWES deployment across multiple facilities. Validating platform scalability across diverse logistics operators.
Partnership

Growth Phase

Announced expanded GWES roadmap including advanced AI modules, third-party integrations, and international deployment pathway. Accelerated customer acquisition underway.
