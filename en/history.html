Our Journey

Transforming Logistics Through Technology

A decade of pioneering the future of logistics with robotics and AI. From startup to leading logistics DX company.

The logistics industry has long faced structural challenges: labor shortages and manual operations. GROUND was founded to solve these from the ground up with AI, mathematical optimization, and robotics.

Starting with overseas robot distribution, then pivoting to proprietary AI software development, and culminating in the creation of GWES — an integrated logistics facility management system. This decade-long journey is the story of logistics DX itself.

Chapter 0
2012–14
Prehistory — Early Signs
Amazon's major robotics acquisition. Japan begins exploring warehouse robotics. The seeds that would grow into GROUND.
2012
Amazon acquires Kiva Systems for $775M
The dawn of an era where robotics becomes central to warehouse operations. It became clear that Japan's logistics industry needed similar technological innovation.
INDUSTRY
2012–2014
Rakuten Logistics co-develops logistics robots with Hitachi
After extensive prototyping, "Racrew" was unveiled in 2014. The practical application of logistics robotics in Japan had begun. This experience and knowledge would later lead to the founding of GROUND.
PREHISTORY
Chapter 1
2015–19
Foundation & Challenge
A logistics tech company is born. Bringing Indian robots to Japan. From overseas partnerships to the decision to develop proprietary AI software.
2015.04
GROUND Inc. Founded
Established to realize "Intelligent Logistics" through the fusion of technology and logistics. Launched as a Japan-based logistics tech company, drawing on Rakuten Logistics experience and the global robotics wave.
FOUNDED
2016.01
Strategic Partnership with GreyOrange
Formed a strategic partnership with Indian robotics firm GreyOrange. Secured exclusive rights to sell the "Butler®" logistics robot in Japan.
PARTNERSHIP
2016.05
Capital Alliance with Okamura
Formed a capital and business alliance with office and logistics equipment giant Okamura. The beginning of a partnership that would deepen profoundly a decade later.
PARTNERSHIP
2017.06
Capital Alliance with Daiwa House Industry
Capital and business alliance with logistics facility leader Daiwa House Industry. Received ¥1 billion investment, accelerating robot deployment in logistics facilities.
FUNDING
2017.12
Butler® First Deployment — Nitori Group
The logistics robot "Butler®" began operations at Nitori Group's logistics center — the first deployment in Japan. A new era for logistics robotics in the country.
DEPLOYMENT

Robot distribution alone cannot solve the fundamental challenges of logistics. We need the power of software to optimize entire warehouses.

2018.08
Strategic Partnership with HIT ROBOT GROUP (China)
Formed strategic partnership with HRG, a robotics company from Harbin Institute of Technology. Expanded global robot procurement network.
PARTNERSHIP
2019.03
DyAS Patent Granted & AMR Deployment
DyAS inventory placement optimization algorithm granted a patent. AMR robots deployed at Diamond Head in July of the same year.
PRODUCT
2019.08
¥1.71 Billion Series B Funding
Raised ¥1.71 billion from INCJ, Sony Innovation Fund, Sapphire Capital, JA Mitsui Leasing and others.
FUNDING
2018.07
DyAS AI Software Pilot at Trusco Nakayama
Began pilot testing of DyAS at Trusco Nakayama's logistics center. A critical step toward the transition from robot company to software company.
PRODUCT
Chapter 2
2020–23
Evolution to Software Company
Building on robot deployment experience, DyAS officially launches. Then its evolution: GWES is born as an integrated logistics facility management system.
2020.06
Logistics Ecosystem with Japan Unisys / PEER Launch
Announced logistics ecosystem joint venture with Japan Unisys (now BIPROGY). Launched the autonomous collaborative robot "PEER" assessment program.
PRODUCT
2020.09
DyAS AI Software Official Launch
Officially released proprietary AI logistics software "DyAS." Patented algorithms enable inventory placement and resource allocation optimization. A major turning point from robot company to software company.
PRODUCT
2021.06
GWES Official Launch — The Logistics OS
Evolving from DyAS, the integrated logistics facility management system "GWES" officially launches. Comprising 13 modules, this "Logistics OS" becomes GROUND's core product.
PRODUCT
2021
Capital Alliance with Trusco Nakayama
Received ¥500 million investment from professional tool distributor Trusco Nakayama.
FUNDING

GWES is not just software. As a logistics facility OS, it's a platform that optimizes human-robot collaboration.

Chapter 3
2024–
Growth Phase
Deepened partnership with Okamura and new management structure. Accelerating deployments at major enterprises including Nippon Express and Kao. The challenge toward 1,000 sites begins.
2024.03
Strengthened Alliance with Okamura
INCJ transferred all shares to Okamura. A relationship that began 8 years ago deepens into a stronger bond. New management structure built for the next decade.
ORGANIZATION
2024.04
GWES Adopted for Nippon Express "NX-GLOW"
GWES adopted for "NX-GLOW," the global standard warehouse management system of Nippon Express, one of Japan's largest logistics companies. Global-scale GWES deployment begins.
DEPLOYMENT
2024.06
GWES Rollout Across Kao's 44 Nationwide Sites
GWES deployment begins at consumer goods leader Kao's logistics centers. Starting with Hachioji LC, planned expansion to all 44 sites by end of 2025.
DEPLOYMENT
2025
10th Anniversary — The 1,000 Site Challenge
A decade since founding. GWES deployments are accelerating, targeting 1,000 sites by 2027. All 13 CX Cargo sites, and beyond to international markets.
