Our Journey

Our Journey

GROUNDの歩み

EST. 2015

A decade of challenging the status quo in logistics through technology

The logistics industry has long faced structural challenges of labor shortages and operations dependent on individual expertise. GROUND was founded in 2015 to fundamentally solve these challenges through AI, mathematical optimization, and robotics.

Starting with the import and sales of international robots, then moving to proprietary AI software development, and finally to the creation of 'GWES' for integrated logistics facility management — this decade-long journey represents the DX transformation of the logistics industry itself.

CHAPTER 0
Prehistory
2012–2014
2012
Amazon acquires Kiva Systems for $775M
The dawn of an era where robotics would underpin warehouse operations. It became clear that similar technological innovation was urgently needed in Japan's logistics industry.
INDUSTRY
2012–2014
Rakuten Logistics & Hitachi develop logistics robot
Through iterative prototyping, announced as 'Racrew' in 2014. The challenge of commercializing logistics robotics had begun in Japan. This experience and knowledge would later lead to the founding of GROUND.
PREHISTORY
CHAPTER 1
Foundation & Challenge
2015–2019
2015.04
GROUND Inc. Founded
Established to realize 'Intelligent Logistics' through the fusion of technology and logistics. Launched as a Japan-based logistics tech company, drawing on experience at Rakuten Logistics and the global wave of logistics robotics.
FOUNDING
2016.01
Strategic Partnership with GreyOrange
Formed a strategic partnership with Indian robotics company GreyOrange. Secured exclusive distribution rights for the 'Butler®' logistics robot in Japan.
PARTNERSHIP
2016.05
Capital Alliance with Okamura
Established a capital and business alliance with office and logistics equipment leader Okamura. Began joint development of robotics-powered logistics systems. The beginning of a transformative relationship that would deepen a decade later.
PARTNERSHIP
2017.06
Capital Alliance with Daiwa House Industry
Formed a capital and business alliance with logistics facilities leader Daiwa House Industry. Received ¥1 billion investment to accelerate robot deployment in logistics facilities.
FUNDING
2017.12
Butler® First Domestic Deployment — Nitori Group
The logistics robot 'Butler®' began its first domestic operation at Nitori Group, a major furniture and interior retailer. A new chapter for logistics robotics in Japan.
DEPLOYMENT
2018.07
DyAS AI Logistics Software Pilot
Launched DyAS pilot testing at Trusco Nakayama's distribution center. Tackled inventory placement optimization and workforce allocation, marking a pivotal step toward the transition from a robotics company to a software company.
PRODUCT
2018.08
Strategic Partnership with HIT ROBOT GROUP (China)
Formed a strategic partnership with HRG, a robotics company originating from Harbin Institute of Technology. Expanded the global robot procurement network.
PARTNERSHIP
2019.03
DyAS Patent Granted & AMR Deployment
The inventory placement optimization algorithm of AI logistics software 'DyAS' was granted a patent. In July, AMR robots were deployed at Diamond Head.
PRODUCT
2019.08
¥1.71 Billion Series B Funding
Raised a total of ¥1.71 billion from INCJ, Sony Innovation Fund, Sapphire Capital, JA Mitsui Leasing, and others.
FUNDING
CHAPTER 2
Evolution to Software Company
2020–2023
2020.06
Japan Unisys Ecosystem & PEER Launch
Announced a joint logistics ecosystem initiative with Japan Unisys (now BIPROGY). In the same month, launched the assessment program for autonomous collaborative robot 'PEER'.
PRODUCT
2020.09
DyAS AI Software Official Release
Officially released the proprietary AI logistics software 'DyAS'. Achieved inventory placement optimization and resource allocation optimization through patented algorithms. A major turning point from robotics company to software company.
PRODUCT
2021.06
GWES Official Release — Birth of Logistics OS
Evolving the knowledge gained from DyAS, officially released the warehouse execution and optimization system 'GWES'. A 'Logistics OS' composed of 13 modules. GROUND's core product finally enters the world.
PRODUCT
2021
Capital Alliance with Trusco Nakayama
Received ¥500 million investment from Trusco Nakayama, a leader in professional tool distribution.
FUNDING
CHAPTER 3
Growth Phase
2024–
2024.03
Strengthened Okamura Alliance & New Management
INCJ transferred its entire stake to Okamura. A relationship that began eight years ago deepens further. Established a new management structure, building the foundation for the next decade.
ORGANIZATION
2024.04
GWES Adopted for Nippon Express 'NX-GLOW'
GWES was adopted for 'NX-GLOW', the global standard warehouse management system of Nippon Express, one of Japan's largest logistics companies. Global-scale GWES deployment begins.
DEPLOYMENT
2024.06
Kao: GWES Rollout to 44 Nationwide Sites
GWES deployment began at Kao Corporation's logistics centers. Starting with the Hachioji LC, planned expansion to all 44 sites nationwide by end of 2025.
DEPLOYMENT
2025
10th Anniversary — Challenge for 1,000 Sites
Ten years since founding. GWES deployments are accelerating, targeting 1,000 sites by 2027. All 13 CX Cargo sites, and beyond to international markets.
MILESTONE

THE FUTURE STARTS NOW

This decade of progress is merely the prologue to a great challenge rooted in the logistics industry. GWES deployment targets grow to 1,000 sites, and then global. We will continue to transform the future of logistics by combining technology with human capability. Will you write the next chapter with us?
